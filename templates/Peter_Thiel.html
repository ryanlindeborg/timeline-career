Peter Thiel

Founders Fund, Palantir, Mithril Capital Management

Net Worth: $2.7 billion

Timeline

1967: Born

He initially pursues a career in law before moving to work in finance and later technology.

For his bachelor's degree, he attends Stanford University, studying philosophy. He decides to stay at Stanford to work towards a law degree.

1992 - 1993: His first job post-school is as a clerk at the U.S. Court of Appeals for the 11th Judicial Court.

1993: After falling short of being selected to clerk for the U.S. Supreme Court, he transitions to a top law firm, Sullivan & Cromwell, to work as a securities associate. Only 7 months after, he decides to leave:(everyone wants to get in and get out quote)

1993 - 1996: He heads to Wall Street to work as a derivatives trader at Credit Suisse.

1998: Co-founds Confinity, a payments and cryptography company. Here's how he met his co-founder, Max Levchin:

2000: Confinity is acquired by Elon Musk's X.com, and becomes Paypal.

2002: Sells Paypal to eBay for $1.5 billion.

2002: Founds Clarium Capital Management, a global macro hedge fund.

2003: Helps launch Palantir.

2004: Becomes the first outside investor and director of Facebook when he cuts a $500,000 check for more than 10% equity of the company.

2005: Launches Founders Fund with PayPal partners Ken Howery and Luke Nosek.

2010: Launches the Thiel Fellowship, a 2-year program which provides young entrepreneurs 22 years old or younger $100,000 to drop out of school and pursue their idea.

2012: Co-founds Mithril Capital Management, an investment firm.

2012: Sells most of his Facebook stake after the company holds its IPO.

Present: He currently serves as a partner at the Founders Fund, a board chairman at Palantir, and investment committee chairman of Mithril.
