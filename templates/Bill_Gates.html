2000: Assumes role of Chief Software Architect, as Steve Ballmer assumes role of Microsoft CEO. Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation is created.

2006: Helps launch TerraPower, a company that aims to provide the world with a more affordable, secure and environmentally friendly form of nuclear energy.

2008: Leaves his daily job at Microsoft to focus his time and effort on his other pursuits.

2010: Challenges the global health community to declare this the Decade of Vaccines, pledging $10 billion over the next 10 years to help research, develop, and deliver vaccines for the world's poorest countries.

2010: Launches the Giving Pledge with Melinda Gates and Warren Buffett, which is a commitment by the world's wealthiest people to dedicate most of their wealth to philanthropy.

2013: Helps launch a $5.5 billion effort to eradicate polio by 2018.

2014: Steps down as chairman of Microsoft, and remains on the board and serves as technology advisor.

Present: He currently serves as CEO of the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, and board member and technology adviser to Microsoft.
